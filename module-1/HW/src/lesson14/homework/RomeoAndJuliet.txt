Romeo and Juliet is a tragic play written by William Shakespeare. It tells the story of two young lovers from feuding families in Verona, Italy. Their love is intense and passionate, but societal pressures and family conflicts ultimately lead to their untimely deaths. The play explores themes of love, fate, and the consequences of hatred. It remains one of Shakespeare's most famous and frequently performed works, captivating audiences with its timeless tale of romance and tragedy.
